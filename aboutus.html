About us

we are Ofer Levinger (mechanical engineer) and Jonathan Nathan (physicist). Our interest in 3d printers started out as a challenge - to build a 3d printer from scratch. After building two different printers, we discovered OpenSCAD - a programming language that allows creation of 3d designs.

Both of us immediately connected with the programmatic approach to 3d design, and enjoyed very much creating templates for our own use. At a certain point, we decided to share these templates with the 3d printing community (for free!) in this site. We use a free wonderful library called OpenJSCAD (OpenSCAD for javascript) to render and calculate our templates in the browser.

This site includes some features never-seen-before in the 3d printing community such as:

  • Various text templates featuring a large variety of fonts in different languages. The templates currently support English, Arabic(!) and Hebrew(!!).
  • Different types of transmissions, including Beveled Gears!
  • Free usage, no questions asked, download STL files suitable for printing on your own printer or with a commercial printing service.
In addition to writing parametric templates for 3d printed objects, we both work also on several alternative education research projects with high-school students.
